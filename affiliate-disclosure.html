What Are Affiliate Links?

Affiliate links are special tracking URLs provided by companies or affiliate networks. When you click an affiliate link on our site and subsequently make a purchase or sign up for a service, the company knows the referral came from us and pays us a small commission. This is a standard practice across the internet and is how many independent review sites sustain themselves.

Importantly, using an affiliate link does not cost you anything extra. The price you pay is exactly the same whether you use our link or go directly to the company's website. In some cases, affiliate links may even include special discounts or extended trial periods.

How Affiliate Revenue Supports Our Work

Running an independent review site requires significant time and resources. Affiliate commissions help us cover:

  • Subscription Costs: We pay for premium plans of the tools we review to provide thorough, hands-on evaluations
  • Hosting and Infrastructure: Server costs, domain registration, CDN services, and security measures
  • Research Time: Each review involves 40+ hours of testing, comparison, and writing
  • Site Development: Ongoing improvements to our website's design, performance, and user experience
  • Content Updates: Regularly revisiting and updating reviews as tools release new features

Without affiliate revenue, we would need to either charge readers for access, accept paid placements (which would compromise our independence), or shut down entirely. Affiliate links allow us to keep our content free and unbiased.
