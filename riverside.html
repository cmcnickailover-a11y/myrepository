What is Riverside?

Riverside is a browser-based recording platform built specifically for podcasts, interviews, and remote video content. Unlike Zoom or Google Meet, Riverside records each participant's audio and video locally at full quality, then uploads the files ‚Äî meaning internet hiccups don't ruin your recording. The platform has evolved far beyond basic recording, adding AI-powered transcription, automatic clip generation, text-based editing, and show notes. It has become a genuine end-to-end production tool for audio and video creators who want professional output without professional-grade complexity.

Key Features & Capabilities

  • Local HD recording (up to 4K video, 48kHz audio)
  • AI transcription in 100+ languages
  • Magic Clips ‚Äî AI auto-generates short clips from long recordings
  • Text-based audio/video editing
  • AI show notes, summaries, and chapter markers

Performance & Quality in Real-World Testing

Recording quality is where Riverside truly excels. Local recording means you get studio-quality audio and crisp video regardless of each participant's internet connection ‚Äî a massive advantage over competitors that record the stream. In our testing, audio was consistently clean at 48kHz WAV quality, and 4K video was sharp even when our test participant was on spotty hotel WiFi. The AI features are genuinely useful rather than gimmicky. Transcription accuracy averaged around 95% in English, competitive with dedicated services like Otter.ai. Magic Clips automatically identifies compelling segments and generates vertical-format clips ready for social media ‚Äî about 70% of the auto-generated clips were genuinely usable, which saves hours of manual clipping. Text-based editing is the real time-saver: you edit the transcript and the audio/video edits automatically. Deleting filler words, trimming tangents, and rearranging segments becomes as easy as editing a document.

User Experience & Interface

The browser-based approach means no software installation for you or your guests ‚Äî just send a link. This dramatically reduces friction for remote interviews. The recording interface is clean and intuitive, with clear audio level meters and a simple layout. Post-production tools are organized logically, though the sheer number of AI features can feel overwhelming on first use. The editor itself is responsive and well-designed, though complex multi-track editing with many participants can feel sluggish. Mobile recording via the iOS and Android apps works well for on-the-go recording.

Where It Falls Short

File upload after recording can be slow for long sessions, especially video ‚Äî expect to wait 10-20 minutes for a one-hour 4K recording to process. The free tier is quite limited at 2 hours of recording and 1 hour of transcription. Some advanced editing features like multi-cam switching and custom branding require the $24/mo Business tier. The AI clip generator, while good, sometimes selects segments that lack context when viewed standalone, requiring manual review. Live streaming integration is basic compared to dedicated streaming platforms.

The free plan gives you 2 hours of recording and basic transcription ‚Äî enough to try it but not enough for regular production. Compared to buying separate recording, transcription, and clipping tools, Riverside consolidates $50-100/mo of services into one affordable package.
